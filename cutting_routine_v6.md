# 4-Day Upper/Lower Resistance Training Programme

**Trainee profile:** Advanced natural (3+ years), ~91 kg bodyweight, caloric deficit
**Goal:** Maximum hypertrophy retention during cut
**Schedule:** Mon / Tue / Thu / Fri (Upper A → Lower A → Upper B → Lower B)

Research-optimized rep ranges, RPE per set, exercise-specific reasoning.

---

## Upper A — Strength Focus (Chest + Back)

### Pre-Workout Dynamic Warm-Up

1. Brisk walk / incline treadmill — 3 min (raise core temp)
2. Arm circles forward + backward — 30s each direction (shoulder joint)
3. Band pull-aparts — 15 reps (scapular activation)
4. Incline push-ups hands on bench — 10 reps slow (chest activation at ~40% BW, NOT full push-ups at 91 kg)
5. Cat-cow + thoracic rotations — 5 each (spine mobility for pressing/pulling)

---

### 1. Dumbbell Incline Bench Press

> REP RANGE: 5–8. Compound press — SBS recommends 5–10 for pressing to maintain form quality. At 91 kg, going under 5 reps with DBs is unstable. 5–8 maximizes mechanical tension while keeping shoulders safe.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 12.5 kg | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Light groove |
| 25 kg | 5 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Ramp |
| 42.5 kg | 5–8 | 2.5 min | 7 | WORKING | RPE 7 = ~3 RIR. First set conservative — gauge your day. |
| 42.5 kg | 5–8 | 2.5 min | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8 = ~2 RIR. Harder but not grinding reps. |
| 42.5 kg | 5–8 | 2.5 min | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9 = 1–2 RIR. Push this last set. Stop when form breaks. |

> Progression: when you hit 8-8-8, go to 45 kg.

---

### 2. Bar Lateral Pulldown

> REP RANGE: 6–10. SBS recommends 5–10 for pull-ups/pulldowns. Lower reps maintain form (no kipping). Higher than 10 = momentum and bicep dominance. 6–10 keeps lats as primary mover.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 35 kg | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Light groove |
| 60 kg | 5 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Intermediate ramp — bridge the gap to working weight. |
| 90 kg | 6–10 | 2 min | 7 | WORKING | RPE 7. Clean reps, full stretch at top. |
| 90 kg | 6–10 | 2 min | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8. Should feel 2 reps left in the tank. |
| 90 kg | 6–10 | 2 min | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. Last set — push it. Full ROM, no half reps at top. |

---

### 3. Lever Seated Shoulder Press

> REP RANGE: 6–10. Compound press. RP recommends 5–10 for machine presses. Too heavy (<5) irritates AC joint. 6–10 balances strength stimulus with joint safety on overhead movement.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 25 kg | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Same warmup |
| 55 kg | 6–10 | 90s | 7–8 | WORKING | RPE 7–8. Stay here and own the range. |
| 55 kg | 6–10 | 90s | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8. Controlled negative, no bouncing. |
| 55 kg | 6–10 | 90s | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. When 10-10-10, go to 57.5 or 60 kg. |

---

### 4. Cable Straight Back Seated Row

> REP RANGE: 8–12. SBS recommends 8–15 for rows — "too heavy turns rows into hip hinge, not lat exercise." Cable row with strict form needs moderate load. 8–12 keeps back as limiter.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 35 kg | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Same warmup |
| 60 kg | 5 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Ramp — different movement pattern from pulldowns. |
| 80 kg | 8–12 | 90s | 7–8 | WORKING | RPE 7–8. Chest up, pull to lower chest, 1s squeeze. |
| 80 kg | 8–12 | 90s | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8. |
| 80 kg | 8–12 | 90s | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. When 12-12-12, go to 85 kg. |

---

### 5. Cable Leaning Lateral Raise

> REP RANGE: 12–20. Side delts are small, high slow-twitch%. RP/SBS: isolation = 10–20+, side delts specifically 12–20. Heavy lateral raises recruit traps, not delts. Cable provides constant tension (dumbbell has zero tension at bottom).

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 15 kg | 8 | 45s | 4–5 | FEELER | Warm shoulder in this pattern |
| 30 kg | 12–20 | 60s | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. Strict form, no momentum. |
| 30 kg | 12–20 | 60s | 9–10 | WORKING | RPE 9–10. Last set — go to failure. Safe on isolations. |

> PLATEAU TIP: Try 3-sec eccentrics if stuck.

---

### 6. Omni Direction Face Pull (with Rope)

> REP RANGE: 15–25. Rear delts = small, high slow-twitch%. Very safe joint-wise. RP recommends 15–25 for rear delts. Higher reps = more metabolic stress which rear delts respond to.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 25 kg | 8 | 45s | 4–5 | FEELER | Feel rear delts + external rotators |
| 40 kg | 15–25 | 60s | 8–9 | WORKING | 40 kg targets rear delts properly at this rep range. |
| 40 kg | 15–25 | 60s | 9–10 | WORKING | RPE 9–10. Go to failure. Pull to forehead, elbows high, external rotate. |

---

### 7. Triceps Pushdown ⟵ SUPERSET with #8

> REP RANGE: 10–15. Single-joint, elbow-only movement. RP: 10–15 for pushdowns. Going heavy (<8) stresses elbows. Triceps get heavy work from all pressing — this is volume/pump work.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 40 kg | 8 | 45s | 4–5 | FEELER | Warm elbows |
| 70 kg | 10–15 | 0s → curl | 8–9 | WORKING | 70 kg at 10–15 = correct RPE 8–9 zone. Go straight to curls. |
| 70 kg | 10–15 | 0s → curl | 9–10 | WORKING | Last set to failure. Then curls. |

### 8. Dumbbell Incline Curl ⟵ SUPERSET with #7

> REP RANGE: 8–12. Incline curl = stretched position for bicep, safe on joints. SBS: 8+ for curls. Not too high (>15) because grip fails before bicep. 8–12 sweet spot.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 15 kg | 8–12 | 90s → #7 | 8–9 | WORKING | 15 kg for solid 8–12 both sets. |
| 15 kg | 8–12 | 90s → #7 | 9–10 | WORKING | Last set close to failure. 90s rest then back to pushdown. |

> When 12-12, go to 17.5.

---

**Volume Audit — Upper A:** Chest = 3, Back = 6, Shoulders (press) = 3, Shoulders (side) = 2, Shoulders (rear) = 2, Triceps = 2, Biceps = 2 | **Total = 20 working sets**

---

3. Overhead triceps stretch — 30s/side (triceps long head)
4. Dead hang from bar — 30–45s (lats, spine decompression)
5. Neck tilts ear to shoulder — 20s/side (traps/neck)

## Lower A — Strength Focus (Quad Emphasis)

### Pre-Workout Dynamic Warm-Up

1. Brisk walk / incline treadmill — 3 min
2. Bodyweight squats — 12–15 reps, 2s down 1s up
3. Walking lunges — 8/leg (hip flexor opening)
4. Leg swings front-back + side-to-side — 10 each/leg
5. Glute bridges — 12 reps, squeeze 2s at top
6. Dead bugs slow — 6/side, exhale on extension (core activation WITHOUT fatiguing abs)

---

### 1. Barbell Full Squat

> REP RANGE: 4–6. Heavy compound, high technical demand. SBS: 5–10 for squats. In a deficit, 4–6 maximizes strength retention signal with minimal fatigue accumulation. Helms 2016: compounds at RPE 6–8 (2–4 RIR).

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| Bar (20 kg) | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Groove |
| 60 kg | 5 | 90s | — | WARMUP | Ramp |
| 100 kg | 3 | 90s | — | WARMUP | Ramp |
| 145 kg | 4–6 | 3 min | 7 | WORKING | RPE 7 = 3 RIR. First set feel the load, don't grind. |
| 145 kg | 4–6 | 3 min | 7–8 | WORKING | RPE 7–8 = 2–3 RIR. |
| 145 kg | 4–6 | 3 min | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8 = 2 RIR. NEVER go to failure on heavy squats in a deficit. |

---

### 2. Lever Seated Leg Curl

> REP RANGE: 8–12. Seated curl = hip flexed position = longer hamstring muscle length. Maeo 2021: seated/hip-flexed curl > prone for hamstring growth. Placed at position 2 because squats don't meaningfully fatigue hamstrings — they arrive at the curl fresh. Lower B covers shortened position with lying curl + RDL for stretched position.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 30 kg | 8 | 45s | — | WARMUP | Warm hamstrings |
| 45 kg | 8–12 | 60s | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. 45 kg at 8–12 = proper working zone. |
| 45 kg | 8–12 | 60s | 9 | WORKING | RPE 9 = 1 RIR. Controlled reps. When 12-12-12, go to 50. |
| 45 kg | 8–12 | 60s | 9–10 | WORKING | RPE 9–10. Last set to failure — safe on machine. |

> PLATEAU TIP: Add a 3-sec eccentric (negative) on each rep. Time under tension at stretched position drives hamstring growth even on seated curl.

---

### 3. Sled Wide Hack Squat

> REP RANGE: 8–12. Machine compound, zero spinal load. Can push harder safely. 8–12 for hypertrophy stimulus without CNS drain after heavy squats.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 80 kg | 6 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Ramp |
| 120 kg | 3 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Ramp — knees load differently than barbell squat. |
| 160 kg | 8–12 | 2 min | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8. After heavy squats, expect ~8–10 reps. |
| 160 kg | 8–12 | 2 min | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. |
| 160 kg | 8–12 | 2 min | 9 | WORKING | RPE 9. Last set push hard. Machine = safe to go closer to failure. |

---

### 4. Lever Leg Extension

> REP RANGE: 10–15. Single-joint, very safe. Quads respond well to moderate-high reps. Tension curve is ascending (hardest at top) = great for squeeze/hold. Research supports 10–15 for extensions.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 40 kg | 8 | 45s | — | WARMUP | Warm quads |
| 65 kg | 5 | 45s | — | WARMUP | Ramp — patellar tendon loads uniquely on extensions. |
| 90 kg | 10–15 | 60s | 8–9 | WORKING | 90 kg at 10–15 with proper RPE. Squeeze 1s at top. |
| 90 kg | 10–15 | 60s | 9–10 | WORKING | Last set to failure — safe on leg extensions. When 15-15, go to 95. |

> PLATEAU TIP: Try 2s pause at top contraction.

---

### 5. Smith Hip Thrust

> REP RANGE: 10–15. Glute isolation. Full lockout at top = peak contraction. Smith machine = stable bar path, safe for progressive overload on glutes. Both lower days include direct glute work for frequency and missed-session resilience.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 60 kg | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Feel glutes, full lockout |
| 100 kg | 10–15 | 90s | 7–8 | WORKING | RPE 7–8. Find your groove. |
| 100 kg | 10–15 | 90s | 8 | WORKING | RPE 8. Full hip extension, 1s squeeze at top. Drive through heels. |
| 100 kg | 10–15 | 90s | 8–9 | WORKING | RPE 8–9. When 15-15-15, go to 110. |

---

### 6. Lever Standing Calf Raise

> REP RANGE: 8–15. Research (Frontiers 2023): Standing calf raise = 5–12% more growth than seated for gastrocnemius. Calves have continuous tension curve. Full ROM critical — 2s pause at bottom stretch, squeeze at top. 8–15 because gastrocnemius is mixed fiber.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 40 kg | 8 | 45s | — | WARMUP | Full stretch at bottom |
| 75 kg | 5 | 45s | — | WARMUP | Ramp — protect achilles through the load jump. |
| 110 kg | 8–15 | 60s | 8 | WORKING | Full ROM = pause at bottom stretch, squeeze at top. |
| 110 kg | 8–15 | 60s | 8–9 | WORKING | PLATEAU TIP: If stuck at 12 reps, try "1.5 reps" — full up, half down, half up, full down = 1 rep. |
| 110 kg | 8–15 | 60s | 9 | WORKING | When 15-15-15, go to 115. Never bounce — controlled eccentric. |

---

### 7. Captain's Chair Straight Leg Raise

> REP RANGE: 10–20. Abs = high slow-twitch%, respond to volume and time under tension. Bodyweight exercise = control tempo to adjust difficulty. RP recommends 10–20 for direct ab work. Use captain's chair (forearm pads) — at 91 kg grip fails before abs on bar hang.

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| BW | 10–20 | 60s | 8 | WORKING | SLOW: 3s up, 2s hold, 3s down. No swinging. Quality > quantity. |
| BW | 10–20 | 60s | 8–9 | WORKING | If >20 reps with slow tempo, hold DB between feet to add load. |
| BW | 10–20 | 60s | 9 | WORKING | Focus on posterior pelvic tilt at top — curl pelvis toward ribcage. |

---

**Volume Audit — Lower A:** Quads = 8 (squat 3 + hack 3 + ext 2), Hams = 3 (seated curl), Glutes = 3 (hip thrust), Calves = 3, Core = 3 | **Total = 20 working sets**

---

### Post-Workout Static Stretching

3. Pigeon pose or figure-4 — 45s/side (glutes, fixes anterior pelvic tilt)
4. Kneeling hip flexor stretch — 45s/side (tight hip flexors are common with heavy squatting)
5. Standing calf stretch wall — 30s/side
6. Child's pose — 45s (lower back decompression)

## Lower B — Hypertrophy Focus (Posterior Chain)

### Pre-Workout Dynamic Warm-Up

1. Brisk walk / incline treadmill — 3 min
2. Bodyweight squats — 10 reps
3. Banded lateral walks — 10/side (glute medius activation)
4. Single-leg RDL bodyweight — 6/leg (hamstring activation)
5. Leg swings front-back — 10/leg
6. Ankle circles + calf pumps — 10 each (ankle mobility for leg press)

---

### 1. Sled 45° Leg Press

> REP RANGE: 8–12. Machine compound, no spinal load. Allows heavy quad work safely. 8–12 for hypertrophy stimulus. Going too light (15+) on leg press = cardiovascular failure before quad failure (SBS).

| Weight | Reps | Rest | RPE | Set Type | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-----|----------|-------|
| 120 kg | 8 | 60s | — | WARMUP | Same warmup |

---

### 2. Romanian Deadlift

> REP RANGE: 6–8. Heavy hip hinge, high spinal load. Placed here because spine is FRESH after leg press (zero spinal load). 6–8 maximizes hamstring tension at stretched position. Going too high (12+) = lower back gives out before hamstrings.

### 3. Lever Lying Leg Curl

---

### 4. Smith Hip Thrust

> REP RANGE: 10–15. Glute isolation. Glutes are large, mixed-fiber. 10–15 gives good time under tension. Full lockout at top = peak contraction. Going too heavy = lumbar hyperextension (compensating with lower back instead of glutes).

> Progressive: 120 → 130 → 140 over weeks.

---

### 5. Lever Seated Calf Raise

> REP RANGE: 15–20. Seated = knee flexed = targets SOLEUS specifically (not gastrocnemius). Soleus is >80% slow-twitch fiber. Research: slow-twitch responds to higher reps + time under tension. 15–20 is optimal for soleus. Standing calf on Lower A covers gastrocnemius.

---

### Post-Workout Static Stretching

## RPE / RIR Cheat Sheet

*Research: Helms et al. 2016, Zourdos et al. 2016*

| RPE | RIR | How it feels | When to use | Example |
|-----|-----|---|---|---|
| 6 | 4+ | Could do 4+ more reps | Warm-ups, feeler sets | First warm-up set of squats |
| 7 | 3 | Could do 3 more. Challenging but smooth. | First working set of compounds | Set 1 of squats at 145 kg |
| 7–8 | 2–3 | Starting to work hard. Bar speed slowing. | Middle working sets | Set 2 of squats |
| 8 | 2 | Hard. Could get 2 more with good form. | Most working sets target here | Main target for compounds in deficit |
| 8–9 | 1–2 | Very hard. Maybe 1–2 left, form starting to suffer. | Last set of compounds | Set 3 of squats — stop here |
| 9 | 1 | Grinding. One more possible but ugly. | Last set of machine/isolation work | Last set of leg extension |
| 9–10 | 0–1 | Near or at failure. Form breaking. | ONLY on safe isolations, LAST set only | Last set of lateral raises, curls |
| 10 | 0 | Cannot do another rep. Absolute failure. | Rarely. Only safe isolations. Never heavy compounds. | Never on squat/RDL/bench in a deficit |

> **KEY RULE:** In a caloric deficit, compounds stay at RPE 7–8 (2–3 RIR). Only safe isolations go to RPE 9–10 on LAST set. Never grind heavy compounds to failure — drop a set instead. *(Helms et al. 2016, PMC4961270)*
